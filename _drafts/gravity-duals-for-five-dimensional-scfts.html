In the previous post, I have recalled that although Yang-Mills theories in dimensions $d \geq 4$ are perturbatively non-renormalizable, they can flow to interesting fixed points that

  • Are strongly interacting (we had to take an infinite coupling limit)
  • Exhibit enhanced global symmetries, as exceptional groups $E_{N_f+1}$ in the case of $SU(2)$ with $N_f$ quarks
  • Does not have any Lagrangian description.
